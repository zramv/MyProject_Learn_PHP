A globally beloved dish, Chicken Tikka Masala features tender, marinated chicken pieces in a creamy, spiced tomato-based sauce.

Origin & History:

Its origins are somewhat disputed, but it's widely considered a British invention, likely created by chefs from the Indian subcontinent in Great Britain (possibly Glasgow or Birmingham) during the mid-20th century to appeal to Western palates. It's a perfect example of fusion cuisine.

Typical Ingredients:

  • For the Chicken Tikka (Marinade): Chicken breast or thighs, yogurt, ginger, garlic, garam masala, turmeric, cumin, chili powder, lemon juice.
  • For the Masala (Sauce): Tomatoes (canned or fresh), onion, garlic, ginger, heavy cream or coconut milk, tomato paste, butter or ghee, vegetable oil, and a blend of spices such as garam masala, paprika (for color), coriander, cumin, turmeric.

Preparation Summary:

Chicken pieces are marinated in yogurt and spices for several hours or overnight, then traditionally cooked in a tandoor (clay oven) or grilled/broiled until tender and slightly charred. For the sauce, onions, garlic, and ginger are saut√©ed, then spices are added, followed by tomatoes and tomato paste. This mixture is simmered until thickened. Finally, the cooked chicken tikka is added to the sauce, and cream or coconut milk is stirred in to create a rich, smooth consistency.

Common Variations & Serving Suggestions:

  • Variations: Spice levels can be adjusted. Some recipes use cashews or almond paste for extra creaminess. Vegetarian versions often use paneer or tofu instead of chicken.
  • Serving: Typically served hot with basmati rice, naan bread, or roti. Garnished with fresh cilantro (coriander leaves).
